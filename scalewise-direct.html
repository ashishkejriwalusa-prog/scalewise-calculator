Geopolitical & Global Economy Analysis

US-China

Strategic competition remains a long-term valuation and supply-chain risk. India, Mexico and ASEAN benefit from China+1 capital flows.

Russia-Ukraine

Conflict sustains defense spending and energy uncertainty. RUB remains exceptionally high risk due to sanctions and convertibility constraints.

Middle East & Oil

Oil spikes are the largest near-term inflation risk for India and other importers. Energy exporters and defense sectors benefit in stress scenarios.

India Rise

India remains a structural overweight due to domestic demand, capex, infrastructure, manufacturing and services exports.

Central Banks

Fed, RBI, ECB and BOJ policy divergence drives FX volatility. Rate cuts support equities, but sticky inflation caps valuation expansion.

AI Disruption

AI supports long-term U.S. earnings leadership, but crowded mega-cap positioning increases drawdown risk.

Consumer Highlights

What to buy: diversified India equities, high-quality U.S. technology, semiconductors, infrastructure, banking, defense and a modest gold allocation.

What to sell or avoid: speculative penny stocks, overleveraged businesses, structurally weak currencies, and any one-theme portfolio with no risk control.

What to hold: broad index funds, quality cash-flow companies, emergency cash, USD/SGD exposure for global diversification and INR assets for domestic Indian goals.

Top 5 Actions Right Now

1. Keep SIP/index investing disciplined.
2. Add global diversification rather than only local stocks.
3. Use gold as a hedge, not as the full portfolio.
4. Avoid chasing hype after large rallies.
5. Review currency exposure before travel, tuition or remittance needs.
